Preparing JPEG images

JPEG images come in different flavours. Unfortunately the software on this badge can't handle every flavour.
In particular there is a difference between so-called baseline and progressive JPEG images.
The software on this badge can NOT handle progressive JPEGS, so these must be converted.
The display is small and memory on the badge is limited, so it also makes sense to make your files smaller.


A simple way to convert progressive JPEGs into smaller baseline JPEGS is by using The GIMP:

* Open the JPEG of your choice in The GIMP.
* Crop and resize the image so you are left with a 240x240 pixel image.
* Use File -> Export as... to open the file save window.
* Give the file an apporiate name, ending in ".jpeg" or ".jpg" and click the "Export" button.
* A new dialog appears, and now you must pay attention:
* Click on the "+ Advanced options" to show the extra settings you need.
* Disable the option "Progressive" as shown below.
* Also disable the options "Save Exif Data" and "Save thumbnail" to make the files smaller.
* Click on the "Export" button.
* Upload the new baseline JPEG you created to a slot of your choice on the badge.



Enjoy!
